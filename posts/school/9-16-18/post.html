What's your pick of the week?

Let's talk about LISts and Processors. Let's talk about LISPs.

LISP is the second oldest programming language, second only to Fortran. It also has the distinct quality of having code-as-data and data-as-code. Additionally an entire program can be described and run as a single data structure. A list.

LISP is quirky, weird, and different, but I highly suggest trying the language. An easy and influential LISP to get up and running is Clojure, a LISP that runs on the Java Virtual Machine. If you ever had the want to use recursion more than you use loops, discover what super nerds mean when they evangelize functional programming, or just go back in time and learn what our forefathers used to use, try LISP.

Author: Matt Nash, Circa 2018

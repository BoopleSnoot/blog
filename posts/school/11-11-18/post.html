Insights on SOLID?

I know this is probably against the grain, but I didn't beleive the articles were that insightful. Some of those designs, if not all of them, could be done procedurally, or better yet functionally. Reading them I began to form the opinion that C++ is considered a difficult language because it is designed to be a used a VERY specific way. The common bugs and incorrect thinking that we've discussed in class are often caused by the user-programmer not fully understanding these specific concepts, and how they're intended/should be used. The whole design of C++ feels... inflexible, and SOLID feels built up to cover, patch, and somehow extend, that inflexibility.


What's your pick of the week?

Google Compute Cloud

So my pick of the week is more of an encouragement of the week. I've been playing around with platform service providers lately, and the one I've kind of settled with is Google. I like their specific options, mainly because their site and documentation appears the most straighforward. Whether its GCP, AWS, or Azure, I encourage all students to at least look into these services because I do believe they are becoming the driving force of application deployment in our field.

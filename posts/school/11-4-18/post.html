What's your pick of the week?

The Gigatron

So as software developers we're often thinking at high levels of abstraction, so for me, diving a little lower can be a fun refreshing exercise. The Gigatron is one example of low level computing theory put into practice. As is explained in the video, there was a world of computers before the microprocessor (sorry Intel). This world was in fact not computer-less. Instead computers were made with gate logic using TTL chips. That's exactly what the Gigatron is. A modern example of a TTL computer. I hope you enjoy the video as much as I did.

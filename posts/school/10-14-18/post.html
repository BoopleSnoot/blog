What was your experience of learning the basics of C++?

Honestly I was kind of blown away by how much I didn't know. I had assumed (and you know what they say about assuming...) that I knew most of what the language had to offer already, but I was completely wrong. It's very intriguing to me how everything in the language (and the standard library) seems to be set up to support a very specific "Software Engineered" way of thinking. It's fun to think like the creators and extenders of the language. It's aloso fun to understand why some of C++'s quirks are the way they are.


What's your pick of the week?

A Breadboard Computer

So, fun fact, I actually transferred from a different college into the University of Texas. At my prior university I took a class called Digital Logic, which as it sounds is essentially learning to build a computer from logic gates (beeps and boops). After that class had ended I figured I knew enough to make my own simple computer on a breadboard, and decided to see if anyone else had thought of the same idea. Of course they had, and I stumbled onto Ben Eater's channel.

As the title implies, he built a breaboard computer, but what's more amazing is he goes through the entire process of making it. He has a playlist where he explains the math and theory between each component of the computer. Personally I think its one of the best technical series ever made on YouTube.

And while I love UT, I am grateful for my past educational experiences, especially when I think back and remember that they lead me to fun ideas like a breadboard computer.
